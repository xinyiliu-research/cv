I am a PhD student in [Media, Technology, and Society](https://communication.northwestern.edu/programs/graduate/phd-mts/) at Northwestern University, advised by [Dr. Yingdan Lu](https://yingdanlu.com/). I serve as Lab Manager for the Computational Media and Politics (COMAP) Lab and the Computational Multi-Modal Communication (CMMC) Lab. Before Northwestern, I received my M.A. in Communication from Nanyang Technological University (advised by [Dr. Shirley S. Ho](https://www.ntu.edu.sg/wkwsci/about-us/profile-detail?id=shirley-s-ho)) and my B.A. in French Literature from Communication University of China.

My research uses computational methods — large language models, computer vision, and causal inference — to study how visual media shape political and scientific communication. I am particularly interested in:

- **AI-generated visual content**: how photorealistic AI images are perceived as real, and how features like surrealism shape their persuasive and misleading potential.
- **Visual political communication**: how state actors and platform algorithms curate visual content, and how to identify their effects on audiences using observational data.
- **Methodology for visual analysis**: developing and critically examining computational tools that let communication researchers analyze images at scale.

I currently mentor undergraduate research assistants from communication, computer science, and sociology at Northwestern, Columbia, and CSU Long Beach.
